    Integrated LUFS — Streaming Platform Targets

    Overall loudness per track measured to ITU-R BS.1770-4, with reference lines for major streaming platforms.

    Loudness Over Time (Momentary LUFS)

    400 ms momentary loudness plotted across each track's duration — reveals dynamics, drops, and build-ups.

    Peak-to-Loudness Ratio (PLR)

    Difference between peak level and integrated LUFS. Higher PLR means more preserved transients — lower means more limiting/compression.

    Per-Band Crest Factor

    Transient preservation measured separately in low, mid, and high bands. Shows whether your limiter is crushing one range more than others.

    Bass Mono Compatibility

    Stereo correlation in the 20–200 Hz range. Values below 0.9 risk phase cancellation on mono playback systems (clubs, phones).

    Bass Energy Over Time

    Low-end RMS (20–200 Hz) plotted over each track's duration. Reveals bass consistency, drops, and problem sections.

    Low-End Spectral Detail (20–300 Hz)

    High-resolution frequency curve zoomed into the bass region. Shows resonances, mud buildup (~200–300 Hz), and sub rolloff.

    Sub / Bass Energy Split

    Proportional energy split between sub (20–60 Hz) and bass (60–200 Hz). Reveals whether low-end weight is sub-heavy or evenly distributed.

    RMS Comparison

    Average signal power (dBFS) per track. Consistent RMS across an EP or album helps maintain perceived volume balance.

    Spectral Comparison

    Average frequency magnitude curves overlaid per track. Highlights tonal balance differences in the low, mid, and high ranges.

    Spectral Centroid Comparison

    The "brightness" of each track — a higher centroid means more energy in the upper frequencies.

    Low / Mid / High Energy Ratios

    Proportional energy split across sub-1 kHz, 1–4 kHz, and 4 kHz+ bands. Useful for checking EQ consistency.

    Dynamic Range Profile

    Radar view of key dynamics metrics — crest factor, LRA, stereo correlation — normalised for quick visual comparison.

    Quick Start

    Drop in your mastered files and hit Analyze. Soundcheck decodes everything in the browser and runs a full consistency check — loudness, dynamics, bass, stereo, silence tails — across all tracks at once.

    Album / Release Check

    Upload every track on a release to see how loudness, EQ, and dynamics flow across the full sequence. Drag tracks into album order and the album analysis will chart the arc, flag jumps between songs, and score overall cohesion.

    Reference Comparison

    Working on a single track? Upload it alongside a reference master to compare directly. Check Skip album analysis — you're not checking album flow, just seeing how your track stacks up against a target.

    How It's Built

    Vanilla JavaScript, Web Audio API, and ApexCharts. No framework, no build step, no server. Analysis runs entirely in your browser — your audio never leaves your device.

    No AI. No machine learning. Just FFT, loudness metering to ITU-R BS.1770-4, and straightforward signal math on static files.
